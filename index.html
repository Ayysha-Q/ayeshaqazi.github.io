About Me

I work at the intersection of digital IC design, computer architecture, and system-on-chip development. My interests are rooted in understanding how systems are built, from instruction execution inside a core to communication across buses, peripherals, and accelerators. Most of my work involves RISC-V–based processors, where I explore core microarchitecture, interconnects, cache coherence, and energy-aware design trade-offs. I enjoy working close to the hardware while keeping the broader system behavior in view. I aim to work on projects that bring together digital ICs and Photonics. This portfolio documents what I’m currently learning, experimenting with, and refining—through projects, research work, and continuous exploration of how efficient and reliable computing systems are designed.
